## Strategic Context for Amyn / IPN DC JK

### Why This Matters

1. **IPN mandate alignment** — This is exactly the kind of programming IPN exists to deliver: professional development, entrepreneurship, and mentorship for the Jamat
2. **DC Jamat demographics** — DC has a high concentration of Ismaili professionals in GovCon, tech, consulting, and national security — the target audience is here
3. **Existing infrastructure** — Farid's curriculum + Jamil's willingness to help = low lift to launch. Not starting from zero
4. **Mentorship angle** — The C-Suite give-back narrative aligns with Ismaili values and creates a sustainable pipeline (mentors return because it's meaningful, not transactional)

### Key Decisions Needed

1. **Scope:** One-off session or recurring series? Bay Area did multiple sessions — DC should plan for at least 2-3 to build momentum
2. **Audience:** Ismaili-only or multi-faith? Bay Area used both. Multi-faith expands the network but changes the JK-adjacent framing
3. **Location:** JK after morning prayers (like Bay Area) or separate venue? JK-adjacent builds community; separate venue may allow broader participation
4. **Timing:** Weekend after JK is the Bay Area model — works for DC too, but consider Saturday vs Sunday based on DC JK schedule
5. **Local adaptation:** The three challenges (Fundraising, Pricing, Unicorn) may need DC-specific flavoring — GovCon fundraising, federal pricing strategies, GovCon unicorn paths

### Risks to Flag

- **Curriculum fit** — Bay Area startup culture ≠ DC. The challenges may need adaptation for a GovCon/consulting-heavy audience vs pure tech startups
- **Volunteer dependency** — C-Suite mentors are the value prop. Can DC reliably source enough senior leaders who commit? Bay Area has a deeper tech C-suite pool
- **Jamil's bandwidth** — He's offering to help, but he's SF-based. Need a DC-based lead to own execution
- **JK coordination** — Using JK space/time requires institutional approval and scheduling coordination that may move slowly
